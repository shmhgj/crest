Pathology

Pathology is a medical specialty that investigates the causes and effects of diseases by studying changes in organs, tissues, and cells. Pathologists analyze samples obtained from biopsies, autopsies, and laboratory tests to diagnose diseases and determine their nature, extent, and progression. Their findings help guide treatment decisions and contribute to medical research.

Trauma care

Trauma care is a specialized medical field that deals with the assessment, resuscitation, and treatment of patients who have sustained severe injuries. It involves a multidisciplinary approach, including emergency physicians, trauma surgeons, nurses, and other healthcare professionals.

Neurology

Neurology is a medical specialty that focuses on the study and treatment of disorders affecting the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, nerves, and muscles. Neurologists diagnose and manage conditions such as strokes, epilepsy, multiple sclerosis, Alzheimer's disease, and Parkinson's disease, using various diagnostic tests and treatments.

Dental Care

Dentistry is a branch of medicine focused on the diagnosis, prevention, and treatment of oral health conditions. Dentists examine teeth, gums, and oral tissues, providing services such as fillings, extractions, cleanings, and cosmetic procedures. They promote oral hygiene, educate patients on preventive care, and contribute to overall dental health and well-being.

Orthopaedics

Orthopaedics is a branch of medicine dedicated to the diagnosis, treatment, and rehabilitation of musculoskeletal disorders. Orthopaedic surgeons specialize in conditions affecting bones, joints, ligaments, muscles, and tendons. They employ non-surgical and surgical interventions, such as joint replacements, fracture repair, and arthroscopic procedures, to alleviate pain, restore function, and improve mobility.

Radiology

Radiology is a medical specialty that utilizes various imaging technologies, such as X-rays, CT scans, MRIs, and ultrasound, to diagnose and treat diseases and injuries. Radiologists interpret medical images to detect abnormalities, provide diagnostic insights, and guide treatment plans. They play a crucial role in medical diagnosis, monitoring, and intervention.

Neuro Surgery

Neurosurgery is a specialized surgical discipline that deals with the diagnosis, treatment, and management of conditions affecting the nervous system. Neurosurgeons perform intricate procedures on the brain, spinal cord, and peripheral nerves, addressing conditions like tumors, trauma, vascular disorders, and degenerative diseases. They utilize advanced surgical techniques and technologies to restore neurological function and alleviate symptoms.
